# GINIT
An app that combines the bisection, newton-raphson, and inverse method to solve for the zeros of most mathematical equation.

- **Bisection**: to have the best initial guess possible.
- **Newton-Raphson**: a way of finding the zeros of polynomials.
- **Inverse**: a way of finding the zeros of non-continuous equations (often including trigs and logarithm).

Ginit secured second place in a regional mathematical innovation competition representing Don Bosco Catholic High School.
